70-Volt Amplification for Commercial Applications

For a large commercial project, 70-volt amplification is the way to go. 70-volt lets you provide audio coverage throughout sizable venues like restaurants, bars, gyms, and shopping centers using a large number of speakers. In a 70-volt system, each speaker has a transformer that allows you to have multiple speaker lines connected to a single amplifier without worrying about impedance issues. 70-volt systems are also easier to wire. Also, pick an amp with a little more power than you need for easy system expansion in the future, as well as for occasional moments of peak demand.

Benefits of a 70-Volt System

The current running through a 70-volt system is low, which means the voltage is high. This allows you to use thinner, less expensive speaker wire. And it doesn't matter how many speakers you connect because 70-volt systems do not have load impedance issues.

How Many Speakers Do You Need for A Commercial Install?

The distance between ceiling-mounted speakers should be twice the ceiling height, so four speakers could cover a 40x40 room with a 10-ft ceiling.


How Many Amps Do You Need?

It only takes about one watt to produce clearly audible announcements or background music from a speaker about 10 feet away from the listener, and two watts in a noisy environment like a restaurant or grocery store. So, a single 500-watt amplifier could easily run 80 to 90 5-watt speakers, covering a very large area for background music and announcements.
